❌ Common Misconception
"CH₃COOH doesn't fully react with NaOH — it's a weak acid, so it only partially ionizes. Less neutralisation happens, which is why less heat is produced."

This sounds logical — but look at the animation. Both reactions go to completion. The answer is elsewhere.
What students assume:
CH₃COOH + OH⁻ → partial reaction only ✗
✓ What actually happens
CH₃COOH does fully react with NaOH — every molecule is neutralised, just like HCl. The real reason for less heat is that the O–H bond inside CH₃COOH must be broken first. Breaking that bond uses up energy — that energy is taken away from what reaches the surroundings.
CH₃COOH → H⁺ + CH₃COO⁻ uses energy
H⁺ + OH⁻ → H₂O + ▲ temperature
Net heat = max heat − heat used to break bond = less heat out
